# PMS–CRITIQUE

## From Irritation to Correction: A Praxeological Grammar of Critique (Δ–Ψ)

**PMS–CRITIQUE** is a domain-specific application overlay of the **Praxeological Meta-Structure Theory (PMS)**. It applies the canonical PMS operator grammar (Δ–Ψ) to the structural problem of **critique**: how irritation becomes interruptible, how mismatch becomes readable, how critique mutates under load, and why correction requires more than expression, judgment, exposure, or explanation.

PMS–CRITIQUE does **not** modify, extend, reinterpret, or replace the PMS operator set. It is an operator-strict overlay that uses PMS as its structural foundation.

Its central thesis is:

> Critique is not an attitude, moral stance, or rhetorical act.  
> Critique begins where a framed mismatch becomes Χ-stabilized and interruptible.

In this sense, PMS–CRITIQUE reconstructs critique as a praxeological configuration:

```text
irritation / mismatch
→ framed difference
→ interruptibility through distance
→ recontextualization
→ integration
→ durable follow-up through self-binding
```

## 4. Core Thesis

PMS–CRITIQUE argues that:

- critique in the strict sense requires **distance (Χ)** as interruptibility, not politeness;
- expression, judgment, explanation, or silence are not weaker critique, but structurally different outcomes;
- drift is predictable when critique pressure is handled without integration (Σ) or binding (Ψ);
- costs accumulate even under non-events (Λ), especially under asymmetry (Ω) and time (Θ);
- publicness does not change critique grammar, but amplifies drift sensitivity;
- productive critique requires Φ and Σ;
- durable critique additionally requires Ψ without coercive other-binding.

The compact formulation is:

> Critique begins where a mismatch becomes interruptible.  
> Correction begins where interruption is integrated and bound.

CRITIQUE_prod := Σ ∘ Χ ∘ Φ ∘ (Θ, Ω, □, Δ)

## 9. Structural Contributions

### 9.1 Critique Reframed as Interruption

Critique is reconstructed as a structural operation, not as attitude or moral force:

```text
not “speaking honestly”
not “being right”
not “calling out”
not “expressing discomfort”
not “judging correctly”
not “having moral clarity”
```

Critique begins where difference becomes framed and interruptible under Χ.

### 9.2 Irritation Is Not Yet Critique

Irritation may generate critique pressure, but it is not critique by itself.

Without Χ, irritation tends to stabilize as:

- reaction,
- outrage,
- avoidance,
- silence,
- narrative reset,
- or judgment.

This means:

```text
Δ + ∇ ≠ critique
```

Reaction is not weaker critique. It is a structurally different outcome.

### 9.3 Critique Requires Distance

Distance (Χ) is not politeness, neutrality, emotional calm, or detachment.

In PMS–CRITIQUE, Χ means:

- interruptibility,
- stop-capability,
- non-fusion with impulse,
- no collapse into verdict,
- no irreversible exposure-as-sanction,
- and no person-fixation.

The threshold of critique is therefore not intensity, but structural interruptibility.

### 9.4 Recontextualization without Integration Drifts

Φ can make a mismatch newly readable, but without Σ it may become drift:

```text
reframing without correction
→ narrative repair
→ endless interpretation
→ analytic displacement
→ commentary paralysis
```

PMS–CRITIQUE therefore distinguishes:

| Structure | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Φ without Σ | Recontextualization without correction |
| Σ without Ψ | Formal correction without durable follow-up |
| Ψ without Χ | Binding risk / coercive closure |
| Χ without Σ | Distance without repair |

### 9.5 Non-Event Load

Critique does not only fail through visible escalation.

It may also fail through non-events:

- no reply,
- no follow-up,
- no correction,
- no uptake,
- no acknowledgment,
- no change,
- no closure.

Under Λ, these non-events can become structurally active and generate:

- silence attractors,
- expectation drift,
- residue,
- asymmetry accumulation,
- or narrative substitution.

### 9.6 Asymmetry and Time Redistribute Costs

Under Ω and Θ, critique is never cost-neutral.

Critique costs may be redistributed through:

- role asymmetry,
- institutional position,
- public exposure,
- temporal accumulation,
- delayed reply,
- reputational residue,
- authority gradients,
- and dependency structures.

This is why structurally valid critique must preserve Χ and D even when the critique content appears justified.

### 9.7 Publicness Amplifies Drift

Publicness is not itself an operator. It functions as an overlay amplifier.

It intensifies:

- Ω — exposure gradients,
- Θ — irreversibility and residue,
- Α — script stabilization,
- Λ — non-response pressure.

Publicness can make critique less reversible, more asymmetric, and more prone to becoming tribunal-like.

### 9.8 Correction Requires Integration and Binding

Correction is not identical with expression or judgment.

Correction requires:

```text
Φ + Σ
```

Stable correction additionally requires:

```text
Ψ
```

But Ψ must remain self-binding and follow-up oriented. It must not become coercive other-binding.

## 10. Critique Thresholds

A central contribution of PMS–CRITIQUE is the distinction between three threshold levels.

### 10.1 Minimal Critique

```text
Δ + □ + Χ
```

A mismatch becomes legible in a frame and remains interruptible.

### 10.2 Productive Critique

```text
Δ + □ + Χ + Φ + Σ
```

Recontextualization and integration consolidate a coordinatable corrective step.

### 10.3 Stable Critique

```text
Δ + □ + Χ + Φ + Σ + Ψ
```

Correction becomes durably bound across time without coercive other-binding.

### 10.4 Non-Equivalence Claims

PMS–CRITIQUE makes strict non-equivalence claims:

| Structure | Not Equivalent To |
|---|---|
| `Δ + ∇` without Χ | Critique |
| `□ + Δ` without Φ / Σ | Stronger critique |
| Silence under mismatch | Low-grade critique |
| Public exposure | Correction |
| Reframing | Integration |
| Binding others | Follow-up responsibility |

Reaction, judgment, silence, and exposure are not weaker critique. They are structurally different stabilizations.

## 11. Drift Typology

PMS–CRITIQUE includes a compact drift typology for structurally recurrent mutations of critique under load.

Prominent drift forms include:

| Drift Form | Structural Description |
|---|---|
| Reaction masquerading as critique | Impulse without sufficient Χ |
| Judgment masquerading as critique | Framed differentiation without Φ / Σ |
| Narrative repair masquerading as critique | Recontextualization without correction |
| Commentary / analysis paralysis | Χ or Φ without Σ |
| Public pillory | Exposure amplification with collapsed restraint |
| Silence attractor | Non-event stabilization under Λ |
| Formal correction without follow-up | Σ without Ψ |
| Binding drift | Ψ becomes coercive other-binding |

These drift labels are:

- descriptive, not punitive,
- operator-readable, not psychological,
- structural, not moralizing,
- open, not exhaustive.

The drift typology is best understood as an **open structural index**, not as a total classification of critique failures.

## 12. Scale and Publicness

PMS–CRITIQUE distinguishes two major scale structures.

### 12.1 Vertical Reach Ladder

```text
irritation
→ framed mismatch
→ interruptibility
→ recontextualization
→ correction
→ durable follow-up
```

### 12.2 Horizontal Scale Ladder

```text
structural / generic critique
→ single-subject critique
→ small-group critique
→ public critique
```

Publicness is treated as an overlay amplifier rather than an operator.

It intensifies:

- Ω — exposure gradients,
- Θ — irreversibility / residue,
- Α — script stabilization,
- Λ — non-response pressure.

Publicness does not itself generate critique or correction.

## 28. Final Note

PMS–CRITIQUE does not tell you who is right.

It explains why critique so often fails even when intentions are good — and why interruption, without integration and binding, mutates into drift.

Critique becomes structurally valid only when it:

- frames mismatch without collapsing into verdict,
- preserves distance (Χ),
- carries non-events (Λ),
- recognizes asymmetry (Ω),
- respects temporality (Θ),
- recontextualizes without evasion (Φ),
- integrates correction (Σ),
- and binds follow-up responsibility (Ψ) without coercive other-binding.

It also explains how critique becomes structurally invalid when it:

- turns irritation into reaction,
- turns framing into judgment,
- turns exposure into correction,
- turns silence into avoidance,
- turns recontextualization into narrative reset,
- turns integration into formal closure without follow-up,
- or turns self-binding into other-binding.

> Critique is not lost by disagreement.  
> It is lost when interruption cannot stay interruptible.
