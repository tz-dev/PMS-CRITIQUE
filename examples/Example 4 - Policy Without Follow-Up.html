PMS-CRITIQUE

Example 4: Policy Without Follow-Up

Case label: Formal Synthesis vs. Missing Trajectory Ownership Scale: c) Institutional Overlay: No publicness overlay (P inactive) Scope discipline: Structural mapping only. No evaluation of policy quality, intent, or competence. PMS application validity remains conditional on Χ (Distance) + reversibility + D (Dignity-in-Practice).

A. Scene Packet (Minimal Unit)

Scene ID: Organizational response following a documented failure.

  • □ (Frame anchor): Institutional policy-making frame: drafting, approval, publication, training.

  • Θ (Temporality): Initial response phase followed by months of operational time without checkpoints.

  • Roles (structural, not personal):

    • Role R₁: policy authors / approvers.
    • Role R₂: implementers subject to the guideline.
    • No role explicitly bound to longitudinal ownership.

Trigger (Δ / ∇):

  • Δ (Difference): Gap between intended standards and observed failure becomes legible.
  • ∇ (Impulse): Pressure to demonstrate resolution through formal articulation.

Expected but missing events (Λ candidates):

  • Scheduled review or audit.
  • Feedback loop tied to observed practice.
  • Assignment of responsibility across time.

Observed realizations (neutral):

  • A coherent guideline is produced and communicated.
  • Initial attention and training occur.
  • No follow-up mechanisms materialize.
  • Over time, reference to the policy becomes ritualized.

B. Operator Mapping (Reduced Signature, Readability Aid)

  • Σ (Integration – synthesis achieved): The guideline coherently integrates multiple concerns into a single statement.

  • Ψ (Self-Binding – weak or absent): No durable commitment structure binds roles to the guideline across time.

  • Θ (Temporality – unowned trajectory): Without time-bound checkpoints, the policy drifts out of operational relevance.

  • Λ (Non-Event – meaningful absence): The lack of follow-up becomes informative: “nothing happens after publication.”

  • Α (Attractor – script stabilization): “We have a policy” stabilizes as the default response to recurrence.

Dependency hygiene note: No standalone claims about responsibility or maturity are made. Absence of Ψ here names missing structural binding, not motivational failure.

C. Drift Classification

Drift class: Symbolic integration drift

Minimal drift signature: Σ present / Ψ absent

Observable drift markers (non-moral):

  • Statement exists without time-indexed ownership.
  • Reference to the policy substitutes for enacted correction.

D. Cost Distribution (Structural Effects)

  • Θ (Temporality): Each unreviewed month increases the gap between policy and practice.

  • Α (Script narrowing): Policy production becomes the recognized endpoint of response.

  • Ω (Asymmetry): Implementation burden diffuses downward, while binding authority remains unlocated.

  • Λ (Non-event load): Absence of follow-up signals low consequence for non-alignment.

E. Rational Response Envelope (Structural Reasonableness)

Within this configuration, the following behaviors are structurally rational:

  • Treating the guideline as symbolic rather than operative.
  • Referencing policy existence to close discussions.
  • Delaying alignment because no temporal enforcement signal appears.

These responses follow from Σ without Ψ across Θ, not from disregard or resistance.

F. Dignity-in-Practice Stress Test (D under Ω)

Risk point: Dignity-in-practice is strained when responsibility is implied but never structurally owned, shifting exposure without binding protection.

Structural indicator: The system avoids coercion but also avoids commitment, leaving enactment unsupported.

G. Reader Guard / Validity Gate

This example does not claim:

  • that policies are ineffective,
  • that authors acted insincerely,
  • that enforcement is morally required,
  • that failure implies incompetence.

It does claim:

  • that integration without self-binding does not generate durable change,
  • that time requires ownership to remain corrective,
  • that policy can stabilize as symbol when follow-up is structurally absent.

Any use of this analysis for blame or prescription violates PMS validity conditions (Χ, reversibility, D).

H. Structural Closure (Non-Normative)

In this configuration, critique reaches synthesis but not commitment. Integration produces a clear statement, yet without self-binding it does not become a trajectory. Over time, absence of follow-up teaches the organization how seriously the policy is meant to be taken. What persists is not confusion, but a stable symbolic equilibrium: the guideline exists, the failure recurs, and reference replaces responsibility. The drift stabilizes where Σ is decoupled from Ψ across Θ, allowing coherence without consequence.
